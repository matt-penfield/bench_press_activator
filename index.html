Persona Reference

Understanding each persona helps you build balanced sales teams. The strongest teams combine complementary strengths — pairing people who create opportunity with those who qualify it, and those who challenge thinking with those who build trust.

Activator "Growth comes from creating opportunity."

What they do: Activators manufacture demand. They proactively reach out, build broad networks, and systematically create pipeline. They don't wait for the phone to ring.

Strength on a team: Keeps momentum high. Ensures the team always has new conversations and opportunities in play.

Risk without balance: Can spread too thin or push deals forward before they're properly qualified.

Pair with

  • R Realist — Grounds the Activator's momentum with deal qualification, making sure the right opportunities get pursued.
  • E Expert — Adds technical depth behind the Activator's outreach, so new conversations convert into credible engagements.
Confidant "People buy from people they trust."

What they do: Confidants build deep, personal relationships. Clients trust them completely — they're often the first call when something matters.

Strength on a team: Opens doors others can't. Creates stickiness in accounts and ensures the client feels heard and supported.

Risk without balance: Relationships stay single-threaded. Avoids tough conversations that could unlock expansion.

Pair with

  • A Activator — Turns deep trust into proactive growth. The Confidant opens the door; the Activator walks the team through it.
  • D Debater — Pushes the account beyond comfort, expanding scope where the Confidant's trust makes challenge feel safe.
Expert "My expertise creates demand."

What they do: Experts win on credibility. Clients hire them because they're the smartest person in the room on a given domain. They deliver brilliantly once engaged.

Strength on a team: Converts interest into confidence. When an Expert speaks, the client believes the team can deliver.

Risk without balance: Reactive — waits for clients to recognize a need rather than creating urgency. Misses growth that isn't inbound.

Pair with

  • A Activator — Creates the conversations the Expert needs. Converts reactive expertise into proactive demand generation.
  • D Debater — Stress-tests the Expert's recommendations, sharpening positioning and making pitches more defensible.
Debater "The client is wrong — and I'll prove it."

What they do: Debaters challenge conventional thinking. They push clients past their assumptions and into better decisions. The best clients say "they changed how we think."

Strength on a team: Elevates conversations from transactional to strategic. Creates differentiation that wins competitive deals.

Risk without balance: Can alienate stakeholders who aren't ready to be challenged. Comes across as arrogant without relational trust.

Pair with

  • C Confidant — Wraps the Debater's challenge in relationship trust, so clients feel pushed in a safe way.
  • E Expert — Backs provocative thinking with evidence and depth, making bold claims credible rather than just bold.
Realist "Trust comes from honesty."

What they do: Realists tell uncomfortable truths. They refuse to overpromise, protect delivery quality, and ensure expectations are clear from day one.

Strength on a team: Protects margin and reputation. Clients say "they're the only ones who tell us the truth" — this builds long-term credibility.

Risk without balance: Overly conservative. Can kill deals that were worth pursuing or slow momentum with excessive caution.

Pair with

  • A Activator — Prevents over-caution from stalling pipeline. The Activator drives momentum on deals the Realist has qualified.
  • C Confidant — Softens the Realist's directness with warmth, ensuring honesty lands as care rather than pessimism.
